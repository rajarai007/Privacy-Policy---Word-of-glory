1 Summary

Words of Glory: Puzzle Journey ("the App", "we", "our") is a single-player word puzzle game built to collect no personal information from you.

Everything you do in the game — your level progress, coin balance, found-words archive, sound and music preferences, and chosen language — lives only on your device. None of it is ever sent to us, and we have no way to read it.

Uninstalling the App permanently erases all of that data from your device.

2 Data we collect

We do not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data.

The App does not:

  • Ask you to create an account or sign in.
  • Ask for your name, email, phone number, or any contact details.
  • Use analytics, crash-reporting, or advertising SDKs.
  • Connect to any server we operate.
  • Track your location.
  • Access your contacts, photos, microphone, camera, or files.
  • Use cookies or any web-tracking technology.

3 Data stored locally on your device

So the game can function, the App saves the following on your device using its standard local storage (Hive):

  • Audio preferences (sound effects on/off, music on/off, volume levels).
  • Chosen language (English, Spanish, or Turkish).
  • Your current level and which levels you've completed.
  • Coin balance.
  • An archive of words you've found in completed levels.
  • A record of any in-app purchases you've made, so the App remembers them.

This information never leaves your device, and we cannot access it. You can wipe all of it instantly by uninstalling the App.

4 In-app purchases

The App offers optional in-app purchases (coin packs and other items) through Google Play Billing on Android or the Apple App Store In-App Purchase system on iOS.

When you buy something:

  • The transaction is handled entirely by Google or Apple. We never see, receive, or store your payment card details, billing address, or Google/Apple account information.
  • Google and Apple may collect transaction data under their own policies — see Google Play and Apple.
  • The App receives only a confirmation that a purchase completed, which it stores locally so it can grant you the coins or feature you bought.

5 Permissions the App requests

On Android, the App declares the standard INTERNET permission for one reason only: the Google Play Billing library needs it to verify purchases with Google. The App itself makes no other network requests.

No other sensitive permissions are requested — no location, camera, microphone, contacts, or storage beyond the App's own private directory.

6 Children's privacy

The App is intended for a general audience aged 13 and over and is not directed at children under 13.

Since the App collects no personal information from anyone — children included — it does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under 16 in the EU/EEA under the GDPR).

If a parent or guardian believes a child has somehow provided personal information through the App, please contact us at the email above and we'll respond promptly.

7 Third-party services

The only third parties involved in normal operation of the App are:

  • Google Play Billing for Android purchases — see Google's privacy policy linked above.
  • Apple In-App Purchase for iOS purchases — see Apple's privacy policy linked above.

No other third-party SDKs are embedded — no advertising networks, no analytics services, and no social-media logins.

8 Your rights

Because we hold none of your personal data, there is nothing on our side for us to provide, correct, export, or delete on request.

You stay in full control of the data the App keeps on your device. You can erase all of it at any time by:

  1. Clearing the App's storage in your device's system settings, or
  2. Uninstalling the App.

If you're in the EU/EEA, UK, or California, you still retain your statutory rights under the GDPR, UK GDPR, and CCPA. To exercise any of them, contact us at the email above and we'll respond within the timeframes those laws require.

9 Data security

Since no data leaves your device, the safety of your in-game data depends on the security of the device itself. We recommend keeping your operating system up to date and using a screen lock.
